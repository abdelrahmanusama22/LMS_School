Curriculum Overview

Elementary and Middle School

At ASD, we deliver a comprehensive American curriculum for our elementary and middle school students, firmly rooted in standards-based practices. Our approach to unit and course design reflects a commitment to innovation, with a focus on transitioning towards student-centered classrooms that prioritize learner agency.

Key Features:

Our curricular areas are regularly updated, placing learner outcomes at the forefront. Utilization of excellent text materials as primary sources of information. Teachers benefit from a wealth of supplementary resources, including our exceptional library. Mathematics Aligned with the American Common Core State Standards, our mathematics program challenges students to comprehend mathematical concepts using models, visuals, and clear explanations of their thought processes. Emphasizing the "why" of mathematics, the program fosters understanding over rote memorization of procedures or algorithms.

English Language Arts

Following the American Common Core State Standards, our language arts program equips students with the skills savvy readers and writers possess. Engaging in meaningful activities, students practice essential skills to tackle a diverse array of texts. They immerse themselves in the writing process, crafting pieces with a clear purpose for a specific audience.

Science

Guided by the NGSS Framework, our science education seeks to inspire 21st-century students to be scientifically literate. The framework adopts a three-dimensional approach, integrating Disciplinary Core Ideas, Crosscutting Concepts, and Engineering. Departing from rote memorization, our focus is on cultivating curiosity, pushing creative boundaries, and exploring connections across the domains of science.

Social Studies

Utilizing the C3 Framework, our Social Studies program is forward-looking, emphasizing applicable skills and deep dives into relevant topics. Students, guided by the four dimensions of C3, become responsible citizens of the 21st century - adept problem-solvers in a rapidly changing world.

Physical Education

Following SHAPE America standards, our physical education program offers a planned, sequential, K-12 standards-based curriculum. It is designed to develop motor skills, knowledge, and behaviors for active living, physical fitness, sportsmanship, self-efficacy, and emotional intelligence.

Music

Delivered through the Core Arts Standards, our music education focuses on creating, performing, and responding. Students engage in creating compositions, present musical work through instrumental and vocal performance, and analyze and interpret the intent and meaning in artistic work.

Visual Arts

Aligned with the Core Arts Standards, our art education emphasizes creating, producing, and connecting. Students refine artistic work, convey meaning through producing artistic work, and relate artistic ideas to external contexts.

Global Languages

Guided by the ACTFL Standards, our global language education provides a framework for diverse students to learn reading, writing, and communication in a foreign language in a safe, fun, and responsive environment.

Technology

Interpreting technology as a natural and essential part of everyday school life, our vision focuses on an integrated learning approach. It is an instructional strategy embedded in the curriculum, empowering students to pursue purposeful learning experiences and develop critical thinking skills.

Learning Support

Following the WIDA and Common Core State Standards, the Learning Support team creates tailored learning opportunities for culturally and linguistically diverse elementary and middle school students, ensuring access to all academic content.

Specialist Subjects

Elementary specialists include art, physical education, general music, Arabic, and library skills. Middle School students may select art, band, choir, and other electives based on the expertise and experience of our faculty.

High School

ASD's high school offers three distinct programs of study: the American curriculum college-preparatory program, the Advanced Placement program, and the full IB Diploma Programme. Each program provides a rigorous academic experience, emphasizing character development, global-mindedness, and an international perspective.

International Baccalaureate (IB)

The IB Diploma Programme, started in 1968, offers a holistic two-year program incorporating six courses of study and three core courses. These core courses, including Theory of Knowledge, a 4,000-word extended essay, and a program of creativity, activity, and service (CAS), aim to provide a broad spectrum of coursework with high academic rigor.

AP Capstone Program

Initiated in 2014, the AP Capstone program, developed by the College Board, enhances college readiness and critical thinking skills. Students earn an AP Capstone diploma by completing four AP courses along with required courses, AP Seminar and AP Research.

Both the IB Diploma Programme and the AP Capstone program receive the “most demanding” classification from university admissions departments.

Subjects offered at the high school level include English, math, science, social studies, global languages, physical education, fine arts, technology, health, and electives. To graduate from ASD, each student must be enrolled in 16 semester classes with a minimum total credit of 24.0 (recommended 27.5).

A mandatory course for all students is "Arabic Culture," fulfilling the Saudi Ministry of Education's requirement to provide conversational Arabic and designated cultural topics promoting awareness of the host country.
